Non-invasive glucose monitoring via breath is one of the most sought-after goals in diabetes care. The Super Sensors platform enables longitudinal at-home breath data collection paired with blood glucose ground truth — the dataset foundation required to develop and validate breath-based glucose algorithms.

Why breath for glucose?

Exhaled acetone correlates with blood glucose metabolism and has been studied as a non-invasive glucose proxy. Collecting paired breath + BG data longitudinally, at home, is the critical missing ingredient for algorithm development — and exactly what the Super Sensors platform is built to do.

Platform fit

  • BLE-connected device for independent home use by diabetic patients
  • Mobile app captures blood glucose readings alongside every breath session
  • Longitudinal multi-session dataset with full compliance tracking
  • IRB-ready data structure for clinical study documentation

With the withdrawal of leading breath ketone monitors from the market, a gap exists for accurate, research-grade home ketone monitoring. The platform supports ketone breath studies for ketogenic diet research, metabolic health platforms, and fasting protocol monitoring.

The market gap

The 2024 departure of Biosense from the consumer breath ketone market removed the category's most credible device. Researchers and wellness platforms studying ketosis now lack a validated, home-deployable measurement tool. Super Sensors fills this gap with configurable, research-grade hardware at an accessible price point.

Platform fit

  • Configurable for acetone and short-chain fatty acid marker sensing
  • Home deployment for ketogenic diet adherence studies
  • Paired meal timing and dietary annotation via app
  • Multi-session longitudinal tracking for fasting protocols

Breath contains volatile biomarkers linked to lung cancer, colorectal cancer, kidney disease, COPD, and infectious disease. The platform provides a standardized, affordable tool for breath biomarker discovery studies that previously required expensive GC-MS lab infrastructure.

Accessible discovery

GC-MS remains the gold standard for breath VOC analysis, but its cost and lab-bound nature limit study scale and real-world applicability. The Super Sensors platform enables pilot biomarker discovery studies and longitudinal real-world data collection at a fraction of the cost — with full configurability for any target analyte set.

Platform fit

  • Fully configurable sensor array for any VOC target panel
  • Standardized breath collection protocol for reproducible sampling
  • At-home longitudinal collection — no lab visit required
  • IRB-ready audit log and structured data export

Building an e-nose requires large, diverse, well-labeled datasets collected under real-world conditions. The Super Sensors platform serves as the data acquisition layer for teams training ML models on breath, air quality, or chemical fingerprinting applications.

The dataset bottleneck

Electronic nose algorithms are only as good as their training data. Building a real-world, multi-session, labeled MOS sensor dataset has historically required custom hardware development. Super Sensors provides an off-the-shelf, configurable DAQ platform that lets teams focus on model development instead of hardware engineering.

Platform fit

  • Broad MOS sensor arrays — up to 5 simultaneous channels
  • Custom sensor configuration for any analyte target
  • Cloud data pipeline with structured CSV export for ML pipelines
  • Multi-subject, multi-session dataset building at scale
